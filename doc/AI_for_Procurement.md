# AI Basics for Procurement

This guide outlines eight levels of AI application in procurement, providing specific use cases and prompt templates for each stage of the process.

---

## LEVEL 1: DRAFTING
**Goal:** Get past blank-page syndrome on commercial documents.
* **USE IT FOR:** RFP/RFI/RFQ sections, SOW first drafts, supplier rejection letters.
* **PROMPT TEMPLATE:** "Draft a [document type] for a [category] procurement. The audience is [supplier / internal stakeholder]. Keep it commercially precise and professional."

---

## LEVEL 2: SPEC WRITING
**Goal:** Turn vague business needs into clear, defensible requirements.
* **USE IT FOR:** Technical specs, service level frameworks, evaluation criteria.
* **PROMPT TEMPLATE:** "Rewrite these requirements as clear, measurable procurement specifications. Flag anything ambiguous that could create supplier scope creep: [paste text]."

---

## LEVEL 3: CONTRACT REVIEW
**Goal:** Surface risk fast before it reaches legal.
* **USE IT FOR:** Clause-by-clause risk flagging, redline prep, comparing supplier paper.
* **PROMPT TEMPLATE:** "Review this contract clause. Identify the commercial risks, what's missing, and suggest stronger alternative wording: [paste clause]."

---

## LEVEL 4: NEGOTIATION PREP
**Goal:** Build your position before you walk in the room.
* **USE IT FOR:** BATNA development, concession mapping, supplier counter-argument anticipation.
* **PROMPT TEMPLATE:** "I'm negotiating [contract type] with a [supplier type]. My priorities are [X, Y, Z]. Generate 10 likely supplier pushbacks and how I should respond to each."

---

## LEVEL 5: SUPPLIER ANALYSIS
**Goal:** Turn data and notes into category intelligence.
* **USE IT FOR:** Summarising supplier proposals, condensing spend reports, benchmarking narratives.
* **PROMPT TEMPLATE:** "Summarise this into 5 key commercial insights with implications for our sourcing strategy: [paste text / data]."

---

## LEVEL 6: STAKEHOLDER INFLUENCE
**Goal:** Close the gap between procurement logic and business buy-in.
* **USE IT FOR:** Business cases, category strategy exec summaries, savings narratives.
* **PROMPT TEMPLATE:** "Rewrite this procurement recommendation for a CFO audience. Lead with financial impact, remove jargon, and make the risk of inaction clear: [paste text]."

---

## LEVEL 7: PRESSURE-TESTING
**Goal:** Stress-test your position before a supplier does.
* **USE IT FOR:** Challenging your own RFP, identifying weak assumptions, finding gaps in evaluation.
* **PROMPT TEMPLATE:** "Act as a skeptical CPO. What are the weakest points in this sourcing decision? Where could this unravel commercially or operationally? [paste text]."

---

## LEVEL 8: SUPPLIER RESEARCH
**Goal:** Build market intelligence without a third-party subscription.
* **USE IT FOR:** Supplier landscape overviews, competitive positioning, risk profiling.
* **PROMPT TEMPLATE:** "Give me a procurement-focused overview of the [category] supplier market. Cover: key players, market concentration, likely pricing levers."
