# AI Basics for Operations Management

This guide outlines eight levels of AI application for Operations Managers, focusing on efficiency, supply chain stability, and process optimization.

---

## LEVEL 1: SOP DRAFTING
**Goal:** Eliminate manual documentation fatigue for standard procedures.

* **USE IT FOR:**
    * Standard Operating Procedures (SOPs)
    * Onboarding checklists
    * Shift handover templates
    * Safety protocol summaries
* **PROMPT TEMPLATE:**
    > "Draft a step-by-step SOP for [process name]. The audience is [new hires / floor supervisors]. Ensure it includes safety warnings, required tools, and a clear sequence of actions."

---

## LEVEL 2: CAPACITY PLANNING
**Goal:** Translate demand forecasts into actionable resource requirements.

* **USE IT FOR:**
    * Labor scheduling
    * Machine hour allocation
    * Warehouse space optimization
    * Throughput bottleneck identification
* **PROMPT TEMPLATE:**
    > "Based on a forecasted volume of [units/orders], calculate the required staffing and machine uptime if our current efficiency is [X%]. Identify where we might hit capacity constraints."

---

## LEVEL 3: PROCESS AUDIT
**Goal:** Identify inefficiencies or non-compliance in current workflows.

* **USE IT FOR:**
    * Workflow bottleneck analysis
    * Waste identification (Lean/Six Sigma)
    * Quality control gap analysis
    * Audit trail verification
* **PROMPT TEMPLATE:**
    > "Analyze this process map/workflow. Identify three potential 'Muda' (waste) areas and suggest specific AI or automation tools to streamline the 'Wait Time' between [Step A] and [Step B]: [paste process]."

---

## LEVEL 4: LOGISTICS OPTIMIZATION
**Goal:** Refine the movement of goods for speed and cost-effectiveness.

* **USE IT FOR:**
    * Route planning scenarios
    * Carrier performance analysis
    * Last-mile delivery strategies
    * Inventory replenishment triggers
* **PROMPT TEMPLATE:**
    > "We are seeing a [X%] increase in fuel costs and [Y%] delay in [Region]. Suggest three alternative logistics strategies to maintain our Lead Time while minimizing cost impact."

---

## LEVEL 5: DATA SYNTHESIS
**Goal:** Turn fragmented production logs into operational intelligence.

* **USE IT FOR:**
    * Summarizing daily production reports
    * Analyzing root causes of downtime
    * Yield loss reporting
    * Maintenance log summaries
* **PROMPT TEMPLATE:**
    > "Summarize these daily machine logs into a high-level report for the Plant Manager. Focus on Top 3 causes of downtime and recommend immediate corrective actions: [paste logs]."

---

## LEVEL 6: EXECUTIVE REPORTING
**Goal:** Bridge the gap between shop-floor metrics and boardroom goals.

* **USE IT FOR:**
    * Monthly Operational Reviews (MOR)
    * Capex justification for new equipment
    * Operational risk dashboards
    * Continuous improvement (CI) summaries
* **PROMPT TEMPLATE:**
    > "Convert this technical downtime report into a business-case summary for a CEO. Focus on the cost of 'lost opportunity' and the ROI of investing in predictive maintenance."

---

## LEVEL 7: PREDICTIVE STRESS-TESTING
**Goal:** Simulate disruptions to build a resilient operation.

* **USE IT FOR:**
    * Supply chain disruption modeling
    * Equipment failure 'What-if' scenarios
    * Labor strike impact analysis
    * Sudden demand spike readiness
* **PROMPT TEMPLATE:**
    > "Act as a Risk Consultant. What happens to our fulfillment rate if [Key Supplier] fails to deliver for 2 weeks? Suggest a mitigation plan involving safety stock and alternative sourcing."

---

## LEVEL 8: TECHNOLOGY SCOUTING
**Goal:** Evaluate Industry 4.0 tools without falling for marketing hype.

* **USE IT FOR:**
    * MES/ERP software comparisons
    * IoT sensor ROI analysis
    * AMR (Autonomous Mobile Robot) viability
    * Predictive maintenance vendor research
* **PROMPT TEMPLATE:**
    > "Provide a technical overview of [Technology Type, e.g., Computer Vision for QC]. Detail the implementation complexity, typical ROI period, and common pitfalls for a mid-sized manufacturing plant."
